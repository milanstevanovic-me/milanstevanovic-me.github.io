Air Engine Powered Food Slicer

A compressed-air-powered food slicer designed for safety, manufacturability, and low cost.

The Problem

For this project, I needed to design a food slicer powered entirely by a compressed-air engine — no electric motor. The constraints were strict: the design had to be safe around food, manufacturable in small batches, low part count, and built under a $100 cost cap. It also had to integrate with an existing dual-action two-stroke air engine's flywheel/piston interface, rather than being a standalone design.

What I Built

I engineered a fully constrained mechanical system with an adjustable, lockable slice-thickness mechanism and enough stroke length to cut common foods. I produced complete manufacturing-ready documentation, including detailed part and assembly drawings, tolerances, a bill of materials (BOM), inspection criteria, and engineering change notices (ECNs), applying DFMA (Design for Manufacturing and Assembly) principles throughout to support repeatable small-batch production.
