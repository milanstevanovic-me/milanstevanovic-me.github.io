SolidWorks Robot Design

A fully constrained 20+ component robot assembly,
designed and documented for manufacturing.

Overview

This project was completed in a team of three as part of a CAD design course. The goal was to design a functioning robot assembly from scratch, using SolidWorks to model every component, constrain the full assembly with realistic mates, and produce manufacturing-ready 2D drawings — all while applying proper GD&T (Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing) practices.

My Role

I led the design of [describe your specific subsystem here — e.g. the drivetrain, gripper, or chassis], working closely with my teammates to ensure all interfacing components fit together correctly. I was responsible for creating the detailed 2D drawings with GD&T callouts for my parts, and contributed to the final video presentation explaining our design decisions.

Design Process

  • Researched existing robot designs and mechanisms to inform our concept
  • Modeled 20+ individual components in SolidWorks, ensuring each part was manufacturable
  • Built a fully constrained assembly with appropriate mates (concentric, coincident, distance, etc.)
  • Applied GD&T standards to critical dimensions on 2D drawings
  • Iterated on the design based on interference checks and assembly feasibility
  • Presented the final design and process in a team video walkthrough

Key Takeaways

This project strengthened my ability to design for manufacturability, collaborate on a shared assembly across a team, and communicate engineering intent clearly through properly toleranced drawings — a skill directly applicable to real-world product development.
